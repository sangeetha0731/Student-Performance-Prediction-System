What We Analyse

Every input chosen
for maximum accuracy

These 10 features were selected from 32 candidates based on their statistical correlation with final grade outcomes.

📊
First Period Grade (G1)

Your grade at the end of the first term. The single strongest early signal of where a student will finish. A high G1 sets a trajectory that is very hard to reverse.

Very High Impact
📈
Second Period Grade (G2)

The most predictive single feature in the model. By the second term, patterns are cemented. G2 alone explains the majority of variance in final grade outcomes.

Highest Impact
⏱️
Weekly Study Time

How many hours per week a student dedicates to studying outside school. Students studying 5+ hours per week score on average 2.1 points higher than those studying under 2 hours.

Moderate Impact
⚠️
Past Class Failures

The number of previously failed classes is a strong negative predictor. Even a single past failure correlates with a drop of approximately 1.8 grade points in the final result.

High Negative Impact
🏫
School Absences

Total number of days missed. Each additional 5 absences correlates with roughly 0.4 points lost on the final grade. Consistent attendance is one of the most actionable variables.

Moderate Negative Impact
🎓
Wants Higher Education

Students who aspire to pursue higher education consistently outperform peers who don't. Goal-orientation is a strong motivational proxy that the model picks up clearly.

Moderate Impact
🌐
Internet at Home

Access to internet at home has a measurable positive effect on academic performance, likely reflecting better access to learning resources and research tools.

Mild Impact
🤝
School Support

Whether the student receives extra educational support from the school. Particularly impactful for students below average — it can be the difference between passing and failing.

Mild-Moderate Impact
💚
Health Status

Self-reported health on a 1–5 scale. Poor health correlates with higher absences and lower grades. Students reporting health ≥ 4 score noticeably higher on average.

Mild-Moderate Impact
🏠
Family Relationship Quality

Quality of family relationships on a 1–5 scale. A stable home environment consistently supports academic performance, particularly in reducing chronic absenteeism.

Mild Impact
